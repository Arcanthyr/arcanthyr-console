### Sentencing first offenders - Tasmanian principles
[DOMAIN: Criminal Law]
[CITATION: Sentencing first offenders - principles and approach [procedure]]
[TITLE: Sentencing first offenders - Tasmanian principles]
[CATEGORY: procedure]
[SOURCE_TYPE: procedure]

In Tasmania, sentencing a first offender requires careful consideration of rehabilitation as a primary purpose alongside general deterrence. Key principles: (1) Prior good character is a significant mitigating factor - R v Verdins [2007] VSCA 102 principles apply in Tasmania. (2) A first offence does not automatically warrant a non-custodial sentence - the nature and gravity of the offence remains paramount. (3) The Sentencing Act 1997 (Tas) s 3 purposes apply: punishment, deterrence, rehabilitation, denunciation, community protection. (4) Suspended sentences and community service orders are available tools for first offenders where imprisonment would be disproportionate. (5) Youth and immaturity are additional mitigating factors under s 11 Sentencing Act. Relevant cases: DPP v Lawrence [2004] TASSC - rehabilitation weight for first offenders; R v Ioannou [2014] TASCCA - balancing deterrence against rehabilitation.
