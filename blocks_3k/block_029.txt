## Family Court Orders

Family court order defined in FV act as one made under Part 7 (children) of the Family Law Act. There are other types of orders from other parts that are made, so be sure to check.

Family law act powers of inconsistency (eg. 68 onwards) are also limited to considerations of Parenting orders under part 7. So can suspend Family Court parenting order for FVO if inconsistent. Per s. 68R.

[Section 35(3)](https://www.austlii.edu.au/cgi-bin/viewdoc/au/legis/tas/consol_act/fva2004158/s35.html), [*Family Violence Act*](https://www.austlii.edu.au/cgi-bin/viewdoc/au/legis/tas/consol_act/fva2004158/), establishes that the allegation that the order was made is evidence that it was made and in force at the time of the alleged contravention. This can only mean that the allegation constitutes evidence that the order was made pursuant to the provisions of [s 14](https://www.austlii.edu.au/cgi-bin/viewdoc/au/legis/tas/consol_act/fva2004158/s14.html), and it would follow, therefore, that the allegation is evidence that it was made by a police officer of the requisite rank. Furthermore, although Constable Goss was clearly not familiar with the technical processes by which he came to act in the rank of sergeant, it is clear that he regarded himself, and was regarded by other officers as acting as sergeant for the shift. This, in itself, raises an inference that he held appropriate authority to perform those duties. When regard is then had to the provisions of [s 35(3)](https://www.austlii.edu.au/cgi-bin/viewdoc/au/legis/tas/consol_act/fva2004158/s35.html), it is not necessary, in my view, for the prosecution to prove the existence of the authority to act in that rank. I agree with Magistrate Bartlett\'s description of that section in *Police v Maxwell*(unreported, 9 August 2012) that the subsection *\"creates a rebuttable presumption that the order has been made, and that it was validly made\"*. In my view, the court should accept that Constable Goss held the requisite rank, in the absence of specific evidence that he did not hold that rank. The evidence does not enable a finding to be made that he did not hold the rank of acting sergeant within the meaning of [s 34](https://www.austlii.edu.au/cgi-bin/viewdoc/au/legis/tas/consol_act/fva2004158/s34.html), [*Police Service Act*](https://www.austlii.edu.au/cgi-bin/viewdoc/au/legis/tas/consol_act/psa2003149/) at the time that he made the order.

### "Acting" SGT -- delegated power

PFVO can only be issued by an officer of the rank of Sergeant or above, or authorised by the Commissioner of Police

There is a power vested in the Commissioner of Police, under a section of the *Police Service Act*, to appoint an officer to temporarily act in a higher roll/ rank, and such officer, so appointed, is vested with all the powers inherent in that roll/ rank. (per s 34 police service act 2003

Yet another section in the *Act* allows the Commissioner to delegate any vested power. (section 8) Police service act

Finally, there is an evidentiary provision in the *Act *that allows a certificate declaring that such a delegation had occurred, to be tendered as evidence of such. (s 95 Police service act)
