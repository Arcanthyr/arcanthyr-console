## Youth Sentencing:

**Dismissal**

Dismissal is the least severe sentencing option for young offenders sentenced under the Youth Justice Act 1997 (Tas). Dismissal means that a youth is found guilty, but the charge is dismissed and no other sentence is ordered. 453 A conviction cannot be recorded. 452F

**Reprimand**

A reprimand means that a youth is found guilty and the charge is dismissed and the youth is reprimanded. 455 This is a formal warning by the court. A conviction cannot be recorded.

**Dismissal with undertaking to be of good behaviour**

A dismissal with an undertaking to be of good behaviour is a form of conditional unsupervised release. An undertaking to be of good behaviour is an agreement by a youth

**Release and adjournment with conditions**

Under this sanction, the youth is released and sentencing is postponed for a nominated period (no more than 12 months) on conditions set out by the court. be recorded. A conviction cannot. At the conclusion of the adjournment with conditions, if the youth does everything required by the conditions, the youth is discharged from the order. If the youth does not do everything required under the conditions, the youth may have to return to court and the court may impose a new order.

Those relevant to sentencing restate the relevant principles concerning the sentencing of juvenile offenders which have long been established by the courts. The principles are that rehabilitation of the offender is always an important, if not the dominant consideration, and that any sentence should be tailored with greater emphasis on the welfare of the youth; the emphasis on rehabilitation is consistent with the broader sentencing goal of community protection ... It is true that the justification for the principles concerning the sentencing of youthful offenders is that such offenders are not able to appreciate the nature and extent of their criminality. ### Key definitions

- Under 18

- Victim of 8(1) or 35(3) POA

- A prescribed witness under s 7J

- FV offences

- Applications concerning PFVO and FVO's

- Child sex offences

- Number of indictable matters under purview of the DPP

- Witness declared as such under s 7J

- Section 8(1A)(a) or 35(3) of the Police Offences Act 1935

- Order made under s 7J

### Relevant Sections:

**Section 4:**

In **[ANY]{.underline}** proceeding concerning a child -- can have an approved support person near-by. Approved by court. Can't be another witness.

**Section 5:**

In ***prescribed*** or ***specified*** hearing the prior statement (written or recorded) of *affected child* or *prescribed witness* can go in as long as disclosed and available for CX

**Section 5A:**

A prior audio/ visual statement taken by police of a child (in ANY proceeding), a *special witness, a victim of 35(3)* or a victim of FV can be tendered as part or ALL of EIC.

**Section 6B:**

Where there is no section 7 order in place, an *affected child* or *prescribed witness* in a *prescribed proceeding* or a *specified proceeding* is to be given by remote a/v/ link.

Only applies to ***Specified** **Proceedings***/ offences!

Assessment report can be sought for a child witness and/or child victim OR an adult witness/ victim with suspected identified communication need in a in a *specified proceeding.*

Ground rules hearing -- adopt recommendations of the report.

**Section 8A:**

Defendant can't CX in a prescribed proceeding.

### DPP's notes:

- Eligible Witnesses, S7I

<!-- -->

- All children (a person under the age of 18 years) who are giving evidence in relation to the specified offence. Applies equally to child complainants and child witnesses

- Adults (a person who has attained the age of 18 years) with a communication need who are giving evidence in relation to a specified offence

- Does not apply to defendants

<!-- -->

- Communication need, s 7F

<!-- -->

- A witness who is to give evidence in a specified proceeding is taken to have a communication need if the *quality or clarity* of evidence given by the witness may be significantly diminished by the witness's ability to *understand, process or express information-* See s7F(2) and (3) for more info.

### Assessment Reports

- S7I A Judge **is** to make an order than an assessment report be prepared and provided to the judge by a witness intermediary in respect of a witness in a specified proceeding if that witness is child witness/adult with communication need (see definition in previous slide)

- In determining whether to order a report the Judge may inform themselves on any matter in any manner that they think fit (7L)

- Order for assessment to be made as early as practicable before the witness may be required to give evidence. Can take 4-6 weeks for the assessment

- This requires an assessment report by intermediary which will need to be organised through the Child Abuse Royal Commission Response Unit.

<!-- -->

- Note under s7I(3) there is some discretion re ordering the assessment in instances where the Judge is satisfied that;

- \(i\) it is unnecessary or inappropriate to make the order, or

- \(ii\) the witness does not wish the order to be made

- \(iii\) the making of the order would be contrary to the interests of justice.

- Examples; 17 year old child, articulate, educated, has given evidence before, have met with them and no communication need is apparent.

### What happens after the assessment?

- A judge is to make an order that a witness intermediary will be used if, having considered the assessment report, they are satisfied the use of the intermediary will assist the proceeding.

- Once the order is made, the Judge is to order a "ground rules hearing" to discuss communication and other related needs identified in the assessment report. Prosecutor/defence lawyer and intermediary are to attend (witness need not attend). The judge will make directions that will govern how the proceedings will be conducted. Any directions will be guided by the assessment report and may include;

<!-- -->

- Directions about how the witness will be questioned/ how long the witness will be questioned/ the types of questions that can be asked/ order that prosecutor/defence provide a list of questions to the intermediary in advance of the hearing/ directions as to the use of plans, models, body maps or other aids to help communicate a question or answer
